Road Signs In Canada - What do Road Signs in Canada  Mean?

If you’re driving in Canada for the first time, you should learn the road signs before hitting the road…

If visiting and driving in Canada is new to you  it is wise to familiarize yourself with the road signs  to avoid any surprises. Though there may not be many differences compared with the U.S. (since Canada sets speed limits in KPH instead of MPH), a good grasp of Canadian signs and rules of the road can make all the difference when it comes to staying safe on your travels.

When renting a car in Canada, it’s imperative that you get to know road signs in Canada and we suggest concentrating on signs that you’re likely to encounter, so if you know you’re heading out of town, our list of warning signs in Canada is a good place to start.

Information Road Signs in Canada

Information signs in Canada are the most frequently used road signs in Canada and are generally used on any type of road to provide road users with general information about the road they are using and the road ahead.

 

  • Begin of a new lane
  • Information about the directions of the roundabout
  • Parking permitted
  • One-way traffic
  • Section control
  • Information about the next exit
  • Lane usage and direction overview
  • Place where you can make an emergency stop
  • Lane usage and direction overview
  • Begin of a tunnel

 

Mandatory Road Signs in Canada

Mandatory road signs in Canada are used where you are required to carry out a specific task, they are not suggestions, information or advisory signs, they must be adhered to and as such are arguably the most important roads signs in Canada that you need to know.

 

  • Cyclist must use mandatory path
  • Mandatory shared path for pedestrians and cyclists
  • Driving faster than indicated compulsory (minimum speed)
  • Turning right compulsory
  • Left turn mandatory
  • Pass on right only
  • Driving straight ahead or turning right mandatory
  • Mandatory lane for trucks
  • Driving straight ahead or turning left mandatory
  • Mandatory path for mopeds
  • Path for cyclists and pedestrians divided is compulsory
  • Passing left compulsory
  • Ahead Only
  • Mandatory path for snowmobiles
  • Path for cyclists and pedestrians divided is compulsory
  • Mandatory lane for vehicles with dangerous goods
  • Turning left or right mandatory

 

Priority Road Signs in Canada

Priority road signs in Canada are designed to clarify who has priority at the junction / road ahead. If driving in a country where you drive on the other side to the road, priorities are likely to the opposite of what you are familiar with, roundabouts being a notable difference.

 

  • Uncontrolled crossroad ahead
  • Give way ahead
  • Stop and give way to all traffic
  • Roundabaout ahead
  • Give way to oncoming traffic, road narrows
  • Warning for an uncontrolled crossroad with a road from the left
  • Give way to all traffic
  • Warning for an uncontrolled crossroad with a road from the right
  • Warning for an uncontrolled T-crossroad
  • Warning for a crossroad, give way to all drivers
  • Warning for an uncontrolled Y-crossroad
  • Warning Stop and give way ahead

 

Prohibitory Road Signs in Canada

Prohibitory road signs in Canada are used on all road types in Canada , often to restrict certain types of vehicles and certain manoeuvres such as prohibiting u-turns or setting maximum speeds.

 

  • Vehicles with dangerous goods prohibited
  • Turning left prohibited
  • Driving straight ahead prohibited
  • Pedestrians not permitted
  • Mopeds prohibited
  • Cyclists not permitted

  • No turning / u-turn allowed
  • Skiers prohibited
  • No parking
  • Lorries - Trucks forbidden
